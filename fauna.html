The relationship between Flora and Fauna

Are Fauna always a problem? Are they Pests? Delve into what Fauna can benefit your garden.


Flora and fauna are widely associated with each other, one often relying on the other for survival. This is why it is important to understand the nature of this balance and respect it. Frequently assumed that fauna can be detrimental to a healthy garden, this is not always the case! Some Fauna can assist your garden in becoming its healthiest. Here we will take a deeper look into this association and delve into the symbotic nature of this relationship.

The Benefits of Introducing Animals to Your Garden

You may wish to introduce domesticated fowl such as chickens or ducks to the garden. These animals can forage in the garden; thereby reducing the number of less desirable slugs and snails and providing you with delicious, nutritious eggs. Other livestock can provide valuable manure for composting, which will benefit the garden with its nutrients, encouraging bountiful harvests. Avoid the use of herbicides and pesticides that can be harmful or even deadly to the beneficial garden animals. Never feed the wild animals directly. This can encourage them to go where they are not welcome, reduce their natural instinct in self-preservation and cause conflict which could result in their injury or even death
